Privacy Policy

This policy explains what information the Hush mobile app collects, how it is used, and your rights.

Last updated: 2026-05-11 · Effective: 2026-05-11

1. Who we are

Hush · Daily Journal ("Hush", "the app") is a reflective journaling application designed and published by Sera Dilara Ceylan, a Certified Co-Active Coach operating from Türkiye.

She is the data controller for any personal information processed in connection with the Hush app. For privacy questions, contact details are available through the App Store listing.

2. The principle: your reflections stay yours

Hush is built around a simple promise: your journal entries live on your device only. We do not upload, copy, read, store, or back up the content you write inside the app. They are stored in your device's local storage and, if you have your device's own backup enabled (Apple iCloud Backup), they are encrypted and backed up by your device's operating system — not by us, and not visible to us.

If you delete the app, or use the in-app "Delete all data" command in Settings, your reflections are permanently removed from your device. We have no copy to restore.

3. What information we may process

We collect only what is needed to operate the app and improve it.

  • Journal entries, mood selections, and reflection history. Stored locally on your device. Not transmitted to us.
  • Onboarding answers (categories, goals, reminder time). Stored locally on your device.
  • Subscription information. Handled by Apple's App Store and RevenueCat. RevenueCat receives a pseudonymous user identifier and your subscription status (active / cancelled / trial). We do not receive your name, email, or payment details from Apple.
  • Anonymous usage analytics — opt-in only. If you turn on "Anonymous analytics", we use PostHog to collect aggregated, non-identifying events such as: which onboarding screen was last seen, whether the paywall was viewed, how often entries are saved. You can turn this off at any time in Settings.
  • Marketing attribution — opt-in only. If you accept the App Tracking Transparency (ATT) prompt on iOS, we may use attribution tools to measure which marketing channel introduced you to Hush.
  • Crash reports. Apple may share anonymous crash diagnostics with us through App Store Connect.

4. How we use this information

  • To run the app on your device and show you the right daily question.
  • To deliver the daily reminder notification you scheduled (generated locally on your device).
  • To unlock subscription features after a successful purchase.
  • To understand, in aggregate, how to improve onboarding and the writing experience — only if you have opted in.
  • To meet legal and accounting obligations related to subscription sales (handled by Apple).

We never sell, rent, or share your personal data with third parties for their own marketing.

5. Third-party services we rely on

  • Apple Inc. — App Store, In-App Purchases, push notification delivery, anonymous crash reports.
  • RevenueCat, Inc. — subscription state management.
  • PostHog — anonymous product analytics. Used only when you have opted in.
  • Expo Application Services (EAS) — over-the-air bundle delivery. No personal data.

6. Retention

Local journal data: kept on your device until you delete the app or use "Delete all data" in Settings. Subscription records: kept as long as your subscription is active and afterwards as required by accounting and tax law. Analytics events (if opted in): aggregated and retained for up to 24 months.

7. Your rights — GDPR and KVKK

If you are in the European Union, the United Kingdom, Türkiye, or another jurisdiction with data-protection law, you have the right to access, correct, delete, restrict, or port your data, object to processing, withdraw consent, and lodge a complaint with your supervisory authority (in Türkiye: Kişisel Verileri Koruma Kurumu — KVKK).

Most of these rights you can exercise yourself inside the app: use Settings → Export my reflections to download your data, and Settings → Delete all data to erase it.

8. Children

Hush is not designed for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect data from anyone under 13.

9. International transfers

Some processors (Apple, analytics providers) may transfer data to the United States. Where required, transfers are protected by Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent safeguards.

10. Security

The app uses HTTPS for all network communication. Local journal data is stored using your device's standard encrypted storage. Optional Face ID / Touch ID / passcode lock can be enabled in Settings.

11. Health disclaimer

Hush is a reflective journaling tool. It is not a medical device, not a substitute for therapy, and not a crisis service. If you are in crisis, please contact a local helpline:

  • United States — 988 (Suicide & Crisis Lifeline)
  • Europe — 116 123 (Samaritans International)
  • Türkiye — 182
  • Or your local emergency number

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. The "Last updated" date will change. Substantive changes will be surfaced in-app. Continued use of Hush after a change means you accept the updated policy.
